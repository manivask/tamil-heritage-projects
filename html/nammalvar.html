G. Nammalvar Biography
01 // Cover Profile

G. Nammalvar (கோ. நம்மாழ்வார்)

Dr. G. Nammalvar (6 April 1938 – 30 December 2013) was a pioneering Indian agricultural scientist, green crusader, environmental activist, and organic farming crusader.

Born in Elangadu, Thanjavur district, he graduated in Agriculture from Annamalai University. He spent his life traveling, researching traditional Tamil farming systems, and teaching natural farming to thousands of farmers.

Self-Reliance Concept

"The purpose of education should be freedom. Freedom is essentially self-reliance. Self-reliance means that one should not depend on others for one's daily bread."

02 // Life Milestones

The Path of a Crusader

From testing fertilizers in Kovilpatti to establishing Vanagam ecological trust, Nammalvar's life was a crusade for the earth.

Frustrated by government experiments pushing expensive seeds and chemical fertilizers on poor rain-fed farmers, he resigned in 1969. He then spent decades developing zero-cost natural farming practices.

1979
Founded "Kudumbam" to promote participatory development and restore indigenous crop varieties.
1990
Started the LEISA (Low External Input and Sustainable Agriculture) network and opened Pudukottai research center.
2005
Led soil rehabilitation across tsunami-hit Nagapattinam farms, restoring salt-damaged crop lands.
2013
Led the mass peasant protests in the Cauvery Delta region against the commercial Methane Gas extraction project.

04 // Publications & Legacy

His Literary Legacy

Nammalvar wrote extensively in Tamil to educate farmers directly. He also founded **Vanagam** (Ecological Foundation For Farm Research and Global Food Security Trust) in Karur to carry on his work.

Ini Ellam Iyarkaiye

"Here onward it's nature" – A core guide detailing organic methods.

Thai Mann

"Mother Earth" – An exploration of our land, soil health, and organic heritage.

Ini Vithaigale Perayutham

"Seeds are the weapons" – Activism on saving traditional organic seeds.
